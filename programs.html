Program 01

❤️ Health Adda

Health Adda is HOPE's flagship community health initiative, bringing preventive healthcare, awareness, and regular health check-ups directly to residents of Navi Mumbai. The program operates through community health drives, school outreach, and partnerships with local health providers.

Health Adda addresses the everyday health needs of families who may not regularly access formal medical care — from general wellness to specialist referrals — ensuring that no community member is left behind.

Program 02

🏆 B-RODH / Centre of Excellence

B-RODH is HOPE's Centre of Excellence — a dedicated initiative focused on research, training, and community-level service delivery in health and social development. The program brings together professionals, institutions, and community members to develop best-practice approaches to persistent public health challenges.

Through B-RODH, we engage schools and colleges in building the next generation of health advocates, equipping young people with knowledge and skills to improve wellbeing in their own communities.

Program 03

🚭 Tobacco Control Program

Tobacco use remains one of the most significant preventable causes of disease and death in India. HOPE's Tobacco Control Program runs awareness campaigns, school-based education, and cessation counselling sessions in Navi Mumbai, targeting students, youth, and adult community members.

We work with schools, panchayats, and health authorities to create tobacco-free environments, support those who want to quit, and advocate for stronger public health policies at the local level.

Program 04

🧬 Hepatitis C Initiative

Hepatitis C is a serious liver disease that often goes undetected until it reaches an advanced stage. HOPE's Hepatitis C Initiative focuses on community screening, education, and timely linkage to care — particularly among high-risk groups and underserved populations in Navi Mumbai.

Through camps, awareness drives, and coordination with healthcare providers, we help community members understand their risk, access testing, and receive treatment guidance — reducing the long-term burden of this preventable disease.
